Traditions of Mumbai

Language

Hindi is the official language of Mumbai and the primary language used in all dealings and public transactions. English serves as an informal second language in the Mumbai and is spoken by a large section of its society. All road signs are bilingual, showing information in both Hindi and English.

Culture

Mumbai’s rich heritage and traditions have been shaped by its position as a historic trade hub. In recent years, the Mumbai has undergone a significant cultural transformation, evolving centuy-old customs to fit the contemporary world we live in today.

Currency & Payments

Currency exchange and transactions are easy in Mumbai. Mumbai’s national currency is the Indian rupees, which is subdivided into 100 paisa. You’ll receive notes in denominations of five rupees, ten rupees, 50 rupees, 100 rupees and 500 rupees; and coins in one rupees, two rupees, 5rupees, 10rupees,and 20rupees

Laws & Etiquette

Dress code is dependent on individual establishments, some casual, others keen on sophistication. Places of worship specifically do require you to dress modestly while visiting.

Send and receive money

You can send or receive money in Mumbai by transferring funds online or through a bank that offers fast money transfer services, all of which are subject to the rules and regulations of the Indian monetary authority
